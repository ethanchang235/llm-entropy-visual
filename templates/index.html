LLM Entropy Visualizer - Help

This tool visualizes the next token predictions of a Language Model (LLM) and calculates the Shannon entropy of its probability distribution.

Features:

  • Text Input: Type any text to see what the LLM predicts will come next. Leave it empty to see predictions for the very first token of a sequence.
  • Top K Tokens: Adjust how many of the most probable next tokens are displayed in the bar chart.
  • Temperature: Controls the randomness of the predictions.
    • Lower temperatures (e.g., < 1.0) make the distribution "peakier" and predictions more deterministic/less surprising.
    • Higher temperatures (e.g., > 1.0) make the distribution "flatter" and predictions more random/diverse/surprising.
  • Entropy: A measure of uncertainty or "surprise" in the LLM's prediction for the next token.
    • Low entropy means the model is very confident about a few specific next tokens.
    • High entropy means the model is less certain, and many tokens are somewhat equally probable.
  • Top K Cumulative Probability: The sum of probabilities of the displayed Top K tokens. Indicates how much of the model's confidence is captured by these top choices.
  • Input Tokenization: Shows how your input text is broken down into tokens by the model's tokenizer. Each token has an ID.
  • Probability Chart: Visualizes the probabilities of the top K next tokens. You can click on a bar to append that token to your input text.
  • Surprise Me: Fills the input with a random phrase.
  • Clear Button: Clears the text input area.
  • Input History: Shows your recent inputs and their entropy. Click to reload an input.
  • Entropy Trend: Plots entropy against the length of your input sequence (character count).

Model Used: GPT-2 (Small)
